# Biomedical Figure Rules

Use this reference for biomedical, biological, clinical, and medical science figures.

## General Rules

- Separate observed data, statistical inference, and mechanistic interpretation.
- Do not convert correlation into causal or mechanistic language.
- State sample sizes and biological or technical replicates where relevant.
- Mark exploratory analysis as exploratory.
- State normalization and preprocessing for assay-derived figures.

## Image Panels

- Preserve scale bars for microscopy and imaging.
- State magnification, stain, marker, channel, or modality where relevant.
- Do not alter image content in ways that change interpretation.
- Apply brightness/contrast adjustments consistently and disclose them when relevant.

## Quantitative Panels

- Show distribution for small biological samples when possible.
- Define error bars.
- Avoid overemphasis on p-value stars without effect size.

## Mechanism Panels

- Use "proposed", "hypothesized", or "model" unless direct evidence is supplied.
- Define arrow semantics.
- Avoid implying clinical benefit from mechanistic schematic alone.
